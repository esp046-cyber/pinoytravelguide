üì∂Internet & Connectivity

  • SIM Cards: Globe, Smart, DITO (‚Ç±50-100)
  • Data promos: 1GB = ‚Ç±50, Unlimited = ‚Ç±99-299
  • Load: Buy at 7-Eleven, sari-sari stores
  • WiFi: Available in malls, cafes, hotels
  • Coverage: Cities good, provinces spotty

üîåPower & Charging

  • Voltage: 220V, 60Hz (same as most countries)
  • Plug type: Type A, B, C (US-style flat pins)
  • Bring power bank (20,000mAh recommended)
  • Universal adapter helpful for multiple devices
  • Charging stations in malls and airports

üè™Shopping Tips

  • Malls: SM, Ayala, Robinsons - air-conditioned shopping
  • Markets: Divisoria, Greenhills - bargain hunting
  • Pasalubong: Dried mangoes, polvoron, barong
  • Handicrafts: Support local artisans
  • Haggling: Expected in markets, not malls
  • Bring eco-bag (plastic ban in many cities)
üí° Beginner Tip:

Always carry small bills (‚Ç±20, ‚Ç±50, ‚Ç±100). Many vendors and drivers don't have change for big bills!

üõ°Ô∏èPersonal Safety

  • Always tell someone your itinerary
  • Share live location with family/friends
  • Avoid dark, isolated areas at night
  • Don't accept food/drinks from strangers
  • Keep photocopies of important documents
  • Trust your instincts - if it feels wrong, leave

üíºValuables Protection

  • Use hotel safe for passport and cash
  • Don't flash expensive gadgets in public
  • Use anti-theft backpack in crowded areas
  • Keep wallet in front pocket
  • Split money in different locations
  • Take photos of important documents

üöïTransportation Safety

  • Use Grab instead of flagging taxis
  • Share ride details with someone
  • Sit behind driver in taxis
  • Verify plate number before getting in
  • Agree on fare before tricycle rides
  • Avoid motorcycle taxis at night

üè•Health & Medical

  • Drink only bottled or filtered water
  • Bring anti-diarrhea medicine
  • Use insect repellent (dengue prevention)
  • Wash hands frequently or use sanitizer
  • Avoid street food if sensitive stomach
  • Know nearest hospital in your area

üåäWater Activity Safety

  • Always wear life vest during island hopping
  • Check weather before water activities
  • Don't swim alone in unfamiliar areas
  • Follow lifeguard instructions
  • Be aware of strong currents and riptides
  • Use reef shoes to avoid sea urchins

üö®Scam Awareness

  • Taxi meter scam: Ensure meter is on and not rigged
  • Overcharging: Know standard prices beforehand
  • Fake tour guides: Book with licensed operators
  • Sob stories: Be wary of elaborate hard-luck tales
  • Distraction theft: Watch belongings in crowds
  • ATM skimming: Use ATMs inside malls/banks
  • "Barker" scam: Overpriced tours from touts at ports
  • Gem/pearl scam: Fake jewelry in tourist areas

üå™Ô∏èNatural Disaster Preparedness

  • Monitor weather during typhoon season (Jun-Nov)
  • Know evacuation routes in hotels
  • Download weather apps (PAGASA, Weather PH)
  • Keep emergency contacts saved
  • Have backup accommodation plans
  • Follow local government advisories
  • Travel insurance that covers natural disasters

üèäSwimming & Diving Safety

  • Never swim alone or at night
  • Check current and tide conditions
  • Stay within designated swimming areas
  • Use certified dive operators only
  • Don't dive if you feel unwell
  • Watch for jellyfish warnings
  • Avoid alcohol before water activities

üöóRoad Safety

  • Always wear seatbelt (required by law)
  • Helmet mandatory for motorcycles
  • Traffic can be chaotic - be alert
  • Cross at pedestrian lanes
  • Watch for jeepneys stopping suddenly
  • Rent from reputable companies only
  • Check vehicle condition before renting
üí° Emergency Tip:

Save emergency contacts in your phone under "ICE" (In Case of Emergency). Include embassy number if you're a foreign national.

ü•óDietary Tips

  • Vegetarian: Say "walang karne" (no meat)
  • Allergies: "May allergy ako sa..." (I'm allergic to...)
  • Not spicy: "Hindi maanghang" (not spicy)
  • Less sweet: "Hindi masyadong matamis"
  • Filipino food tends to be meat-heavy
  • Inform servers early about restrictions

üç¥Food Safety Tips

  • Choose busy street food stalls (high turnover)
  • Avoid raw or undercooked food
  • Peel fruits yourself when possible
  • Bottled water only, avoid tap water
  • Ice in restaurants usually safe
  • Wash hands before eating
  • Bring Imodium just in case
üí° Food Tip:

Try "Merienda" culture! Filipinos eat 3 meals + 2 snack times (10 AM & 3 PM). Perfect for trying more dishes!
